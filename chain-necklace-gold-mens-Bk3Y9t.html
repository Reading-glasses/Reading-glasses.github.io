What is Presbyopia and When Does it Start?

    
Age-related natural process affecting Eye Health.

    
Presbyopia is a natural age-related condition affecting the eye's ability to focus on near objects. It's a gradual process, typically beginning in your 40s, although some individuals may experience symptoms earlier or later. This natural decline in focusing ability is due to the hardening and loss of elasticity of the eye's lens, making it more difficult to accommodate for close-up vision. This is a common experience; indeed, understanding presbyopia and reading spectacles is crucial for maintaining visual comfort. Many people find that choosing the right reading spectacles is key to managing this age-related change.


    
Global prevalence: over 800 million people affected by near vision impairment.

    
The impact of presbyopia is significant on a global scale. More than 800 million people worldwide experience near vision impairment due to this condition.
