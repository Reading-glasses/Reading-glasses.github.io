Monovision Contact Lenses for Reading: An Overview

    
Monovision contact lenses for reading represent one approach to correcting **presbyopia** without **reading spectacles**. In this method, one eye is corrected for **distance vision** and the other eye is corrected for **near vision**. Typically, the dominant eye is corrected for distance, while the non-dominant eye is corrected for near tasks, providing functional **close-up vision**. The brain then learns to adapt and suppress the blurry image from the non-focused eye, effectively using each eye for its designated purpose. This allows individuals to see clearly at both near and far distances without the aid of **reading aids**.

Limitations: Reduced Stereopsis and Night Driving Challenges with Monovision Contact Lenses

    
Despite its benefits, **monovision contact lenses** do come with certain limitations. One significant drawback is **reduced stereopsis**, which refers to diminished depth perception.
