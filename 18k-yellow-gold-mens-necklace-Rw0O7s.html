The Onset of Near Vision Difficulties

Defining Presbyopia: The Age-Related Decline of Focus

Presbyopia is characterized by the "inability of the eye to focus on nearby objects due to aging." This age-related condition affects nearly everyone as they get older, typically starting around age 40. The underlying cause is a gradual stiffening of the lens within the eye, reducing its ability to change shape and accommodate for near vision. As a result, many people find themselves needing reading spectacles, also known as reading glasses, to see clearly up close. This natural process is often the first sign that you may need vision correction options. Ignoring these signs can lead to increased eye strain from reading.
