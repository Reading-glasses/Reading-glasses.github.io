Experiencing difficulty reading small print or holding objects further away.

Decoding Your Prescription: Beyond the Basics

Getting the right glasses with bifocals starts with a current prescription. Don't rely on an old one! An up-to-date eye exam from your optometrist or ophthalmologist is vital. This ensures your prescription accurately reflects your current vision needs, guaranteeing the best fit for your bifocal glasses. Your prescription will detail both your distance and near correction values, crucial for accurate lens creation. These values, along with your pupillary distance (PD), are the foundation for creating perfectly fitted glasses with bifocals. If you're unsure about any aspect of your prescription, reach out to your eye care professional for clarification before ordering your new glasses with bifocals.

Mastering Your Pupillary Distance: An At-Home Guide

how to measure pupillary distance for bifocals is a critical step often overlooked. Your pupillary distance (PD) measures the distance between the pupils of your eyes. Accurate PD measurement is crucial for proper lens placement in your glasses with bifocals. Incorrect PD can lead to eye strain, headaches, and discomfort. While your eye doctor will measure your PD professionally, you can also attempt a basic measurement at home. Use a ruler to measure the distance between the centers of your pupils while looking straight ahead. However, for the most accurate measurement, it's always best to rely on a professional's assessment. Precise PD ensures your glasses with bifocals provide optimal comfort and clear vision. Remember, this precise measurement is key to correctly fitting your new glasses with bifocals.
