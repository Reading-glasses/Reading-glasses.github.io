Traditional eyewear passes through many hands before it reaches you, with each middleman adding a markup. Modern companies like Monocle bypass this entirely. Volume matters. Take Lenskart, for example, which owns the world’s largest eyewear manufacturing facility. By producing glasses on such a massive scale, they dramatically lower the cost per pair. Why pay a distributor when you can do it yourself? Retailers like Lenskart and Monocle have taken control by producing their own lines of spectacle frames. Getting Your Prescription Right

The absolute foundation of a successful online glasses purchase is your prescription. Without an accurate and current one, even the most stylish frames are useless.

The Importance of a Current Prescription

Before you even start browsing for frames, you need a valid prescription from a licensed optometrist or ophthalmologist. Keep in mind that prescriptions have an expiration date. For 2025, the standard is that prescriptions for adults under 42 are valid for five years, while those for adults over 42 may expire after three years. Any reputable online retailer will require you to provide this current prescription to proceed with your order.

An Expert's Warning on Fit

While ordering for adults is generally straightforward, special care should be taken with children's eyewear. As expert Dr. Jennifer Ferri, O.D., warns, "Generally, we do not recommend buying kids glasses online because ensuring the correct fit, comfort, and measurements is best done in person." This is crucial for proper vision correction and comfort for developing eyes.
