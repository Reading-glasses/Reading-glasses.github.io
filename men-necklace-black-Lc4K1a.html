Reading Glasses vs. Prescription Glasses: The Crucial Differences

The key difference lies in the type of vision correction they offer. Reading glasses correct only for near vision (presbyopia), while prescription glasses correct a wider range of vision problems, including nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism. If you only need help with close-up vision, reading glasses are sufficient. However, if you have other vision impairments, you will need a prescription glasses tailored to your specific needs.
